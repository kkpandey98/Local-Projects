Things you should do

  • Every day, wear a new mask (cloth one will do) in public places. More so when in the proximity of people.
  • Always carry a hand santiser and a spare mask.
  • Carry you own food and water bottle to office.
  • Carpooling, usually three people in a regular car or five in an SUV. is okay, but don't use AC. Keep the windows open. None of the occupants should have any symptoms linked to Covid-19.
  • A trained person with a handheld thermal scanner should take temperature at the office entrance. Everyone should use santiser before entering.
  • Person manning the entrance should look out for anyone with symptons (cough, cold, throat, pain, breathing difficulty). If someone exhibits such symptoms, they should be referred to a nearby hospital.
  • Avoid face-to-face interactions; use intercoms, mobile phones instead.
  • At the workplace, maintain a distance of at least 1 metre from others.
  • Practice respiratory etiquette of using folded elbow or tissue while sneezing or coughing. Put the tissue in a closed bin.
  • Use sanitiser after touching high-contact surfaces such as door handles, lift doors and buttons, railings, grab bars.

Things you should avoid

  • Don't unnecessarily touch your nose, eyes and mouth.
  • Avoid unnecessary movement in the office and visits to other stations and persons.
  • Avoid unnecessary movement in the office and visits to other stations and persons.
  • Avoid sharing food and beverage in the office.
  • No handshake or hugging
  • Avoid public transport as far as possible. If inevitable ensure a distance of at least 1 metre from another person.
  • Avoid rush hours and visits to crowded areas.
